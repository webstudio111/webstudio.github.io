This was chosen after researching what actually pays in 2026 vs. what's saturated garbage:

The economics:

- The average small business loses **~$126,000/year to missed calls** (AMBS Call Center / BIA Kelsey data). **62% of callers won't leave a voicemail. 85% won't call back.** They just call the next business on Google.
- The software that fixes this (AI chat + missed-call text-back + booking) costs **$29–99/month wholesale**.
- Businesses happily pay **$99–299/month plus a $300–1,000 setup fee** for it, because it's still 10–20× cheaper than a $35,000/year receptionist.
- Your margin: roughly **70–85%**.

You are not selling "AI". You are selling *"never lose another customer to a missed call."*

**Money math:** 5 clients on the middle plan ($497 setup + $199/mo) = **$2,485 up front + $995/month recurring**. 10 clients = ~$2K/month recurring for a few hours of maintenance. Agencies running this model report $2,000–5,000 setups in higher-margin niches (dental, legal, med spas).

3. **Make a free business email** (Gmail is fine to start: `narmis.aifrontdesk@gmail.com`).
4. **Get a free link shortener account** so demo links look clean in DMs.
5. **Payments:** a free **Stripe** account (payment links — no website needed) or **PayPal invoices**. You only need this when someone says yes, so don't stall on it.

That's the entire setup cost: **$0.**

## 3. Pick ONE niche

Broad = invisible. Narrow = rich. Pick **one** of these and stay on it for 30 days minimum:

- **Dentists / med spas / clinics** — highest budgets, appointment-driven, hate missed calls
- **Salons & barbers** — book everything, easy to reach on Instagram
- **Plumbers / HVAC / electricians** — every missed call is a $200–2,000 job, they KNOW it
- **Auto repair** — phone-heavy, terrible websites, low competition
- **Gyms / studios** — membership LTV makes every lead valuable

Good default: **salons** (easiest to contact, fast decisions) or **plumbers** (most painful missed-call problem, highest urgency).

## 4. Build your prospect list (Google Maps method)

Search your niche + your city on Google Maps. For each business, 30 seconds of qualification — you're looking for businesses that are **busy but digitally sloppy**:

- ✅ 50+ reviews (they have customers = they have budget)
- ✅ No chat widget on their website (or no website worth mentioning)
- ✅ Reviews mentioning *"couldn't get through," "never called back," "phone always busy"* — these people are bleeding money and know it
- ✅ Bonus: call them at lunch. No answer / voicemail = they just demonstrated the problem to you. Mention it (politely) in your outreach.

Collect 25–30 of these in a spreadsheet: name, owner name if findable, phone, email/Instagram, website, plus one specific observation each.

## 5. The Demo Factory routine (the core loop)

For each prospect, ~5 minutes:

1. Open `index.html` (the Factory)
2. Copy from their Google listing: name, hours, address, phone
3. Copy from their website: 3–4 services with prices, tagline, brand color
4. Read their worst reviews — every complaint becomes an FAQ the bot answers perfectly
5. Generate → copy the demo link → shorten it → paste into the pre-written DM/email (the Factory writes those for you too)
6. Send. Log it in your spreadsheet.

**Volume: 10 per day.** That's ~1 hour of work. The research consensus: 100 personalized messages ≈ 5–10 conversations ≈ 1–3 clients. First client typically lands within 30–60 days; with personalized demos, often much faster.

> Also send 2–3 to businesses you already know personally first. Warm beats cold every time, and your first client will probably come from there.

## 6. When they reply (the meeting)

15–20 minutes max. The structure that closes 30–40% of qualified prospects:

1. **Hand them the phone.** Demo on a phone, not a laptop — that's how their customers see it. Let them type. Let them try to break it. (Tap 🌙 after-hours mode — that's the money moment.)
2. **Do their math out loud:** "How many calls a week do you reckon go unanswered? ... What's an average customer worth?" Even 3 missed calls/week × $150 = ~$23K/year leaking. Your service is $199/month. Let the silence do the work.
3. **One ask:** "Want this on your real site? Setup is $X, then $Y a month, live within a week, and if it doesn't capture 5 real leads in 30 days the setup fee comes back to you."

## 8. Fulfillment — how you actually deliver (still no code)

When someone pays, you build their real assistant on a white-label platform. You configure it in a dashboard (paste their website, edit answers — same thing you did in the Demo Factory), and it embeds on their site with one line of code their web guy (or the platform support) drops in.

Options, researched:

| Platform | Cost to you | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **MyAIFrontDesk (white-label)** | wholesale/flat | Built exactly for this: your brand, your pricing, connects YOUR Stripe so clients pay you directly. Voice + chat + texting. Strongest starting option. |
| **Trillet white-label** | $99/mo (3 clients) → $299/mo (unlimited) | Voice + SMS + WhatsApp, ~5-min setup by pasting the client's website URL. |
| **Chatbase / Voiceflow** | free–$40/mo | Chat-only, easiest learning curve — fine for the $99/mo website-assistant tier. |
| **GoHighLevel (SaaS mode)** | ~$297/mo | The full agency weapon: CRM + chat + voice + missed-call text-back + rebilling. Get this once you have 3+ clients paying — it pays for itself. |

Start cheap: first client on a free/low tier, upgrade as revenue arrives. **Platform costs should always be a fraction of what clients pay you.**

⚠️ Two honesty rules that protect you: (1) the demo is scripted — if asked, say "the demo shows the experience; the live version is trained on your full business and handles anything customers ask." Never claim the demo is the final AI. (2) Check local rules on SMS consent (in the US, TCPA) — the white-label platforms handle compliance, another reason to use them.

## 9. After client #1

1. **Collect the numbers monthly** — leads captured, after-hours bookings. Send a 3-line monthly report. This is why they never cancel.
2. **Turn it into a case study** — "Bella Salon captured 23 leads in 30 days, 9 after closing time" — put it on the homepage (`index.html` in the repo root), use it in every future pitch.
3. **Ask for 2 referrals** — "Who else do you know who hates missing calls?" Local owners all know each other. Referrals close at ~50%.
4. **Partner with one web designer** — they have clients who need this, you split the setup fee. One good partner = deal flow forever.
5. **Raise prices every 3 clients.**
